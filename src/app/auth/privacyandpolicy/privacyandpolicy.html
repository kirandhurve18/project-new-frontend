Destek HRMS - Privacy Policy

Last updated on 12/08/25

1. GENERAL, PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This Privacy Policy describes how our Human Resource Management System (“HRMS”) collects, uses, stores, and protects personal and employment-related information of employees using the platform.

The HRMS is developed to help our organisation manage key workforce operations, including but not limited to:

  1. Recording and managing employee timesheets.
  2. Tracking employee progress and performance through inputs from managers and administrators.
  3. Maintaining attendance records, including check-in and check-out times, late marks, half days, and leave details (paid and casual).
  4. Managing leave requests and approvals.
  5. Facilitating a rewards and recognition programme.

This Policy applies to all employees and authorised users of the HRMS. It covers all data entered, generated, or processed through the platform, regardless of the device or method of access.

By using the HRMS, you agree to the collection and use of your information as outlined in this Policy.

2. DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this Privacy Policy, the following terms shall have the meanings set out below:

  1. “HRMS” refers to the Human Resource Management System developed and owned by the Company, used for managing employee attendance, timesheets, performance, leave, and related HR functions.
  2. “Company” refers to Destek Infosolutions Pvt Ltd., the employer and owner of the HRMS.
  3. “User” / “You” refers to any employee, manager, admin, or other authorised person granted access to the HRMS.
  4. “Personal Data” refers to any information that identifies or can reasonably identify an individual, including but not limited to name, contact details, employee ID, attendance records, performance data, and leave history.
  5. “Processing” refers to any operation performed on Personal Data, whether automated or not, such as collection, recording, organisation, storage, updating, retrieval, use, disclosure, or deletion.
  6. “Administrator” / “Admin” refers to the designated personnel with administrative access to manage, monitor, and configure the HRMS.
  7. “Manager” refers to the employee’s reporting manager or supervisor responsible for reviewing, approving, and tracking work-related activities in the HRMS.
  8. “Portal” refers to the HRMS platform, whether accessed via web browser, mobile application, or any other approved medium.
  9. “Policy” refers to this Privacy Policy, including any updates or amendments made from time to time.
3. INFORMATION WE COLLECT
  1. Information You Provide: When you use the HRMS, you may provide information such as your full name, employee ID, designation, department, contact details, attendance records, leave requests, half-day or late mark details, timesheet entries, performance-related data, and supporting documents (e.g., leave/claim documents).
  2. Information Collected Automatically:

    The HRMS may also automatically collect certain technical and usage-related information, including login/logout time, device details (type, OS, browser), IP address, location data, system activity logs, and session duration.

    This data supports accurate attendance tracking, performance monitoring, system security, and overall HRMS functionality.

4. PURPOSE OF DATA COLLECTION AND USAGE

The HRMS collects and uses personal and employment-related information to support and manage various aspects of the employment lifecycle, including records maintenance, compliance, payroll, benefits, performance reviews, and communication.

Data may also be processed for security, regulatory compliance, and system optimisation. All usage is limited to legitimate business purposes in line with applicable laws.

5. LEGAL BASIS FOR PROCESSING
  1. Necessary for fulfilling employment terms (attendance, payroll, performance management).
  2. Required under labour laws, tax regulations, or statutory obligations.
  3. Legitimate interests (efficiency, productivity, safeguarding assets), without overriding user rights.
  4. Explicit consent where required for sensitive personal data (withdrawable as per law).
6. DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION

Personal data may be shared only when consistent with this Policy:

  1. With authorised internal personnel (HR, managers, payroll, admins) on a need-to-know basis.
  2. With trusted third-party vendors (payroll, technical support) under confidentiality agreements.
  3. When required by law, regulators, or legal proceedings.
7. YOUR RIGHTS

You may exercise the following rights regarding your data:

  1. Access – request your personal data.
  2. Rectification – request corrections to inaccurate/incomplete data.
  3. Erasure – request deletion (subject to law/policies).
  4. Restrict Processing – request temporary suspension of processing.
  5. Data Portability – request machine-readable transfer of data.
  6. Withdraw Consent – withdraw any given consent.
  7. Object – object to processing for non-essential purposes.

To exercise rights, contact the HR Department or Data Protection Officer (DPO) (see Section 12). Requests will be handled per applicable law.

8. DATA RETENTION

Data is retained as long as needed for employment and legal obligations. After expiry, it is securely deleted or anonymised.

9. DATA SECURITY

We apply technical, organisational, and administrative safeguards including encryption, role-based access, monitoring, and firewalls. While no system is 100% secure, mitigation measures and breach notifications are in place.

10. MONITORING & AUDIT

Activities such as attendance, timesheets, and leave applications may be monitored proportionately for compliance and functionality.

11. CHANGES TO THIS POLICY

Updates may be made due to legal/practice changes. Material updates will be communicated. Continued use signifies acceptance.
