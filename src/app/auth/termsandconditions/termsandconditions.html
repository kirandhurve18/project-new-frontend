5. ATTENDANCE & TIMESHEET RULES
  1. All attendance entries, check-in/check-out times, and timesheet submissions must be truthful, accurate, and reflect the actual hours worked by the User. Any falsification, misrepresentation, or manipulation of records is strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.
  2. Users are required to log their daily attendance through the Portal at the start and end of their working hours, or as otherwise directed by the Company. Failure to record attendance accurately may result in late marks, half-day deductions, or loss of pay in accordance with the Company’s leave and attendance policies.
  3. Timesheets must be completed and submitted within the timelines i.e. within 2 days of the actual working day. Delayed submissions may be subject to reminders, escalation to managers, or payroll impact.
  4. Any absence from work must be applied for and approved through the Portal’s leave management module, in line with the Company’s leave policy. Unrecorded absences may be treated as unpaid leave or loss of pay.
  5. Overtime and late marks will be automatically calculated by the Portal based on attendance logs and as per the Company’s applicable policies. Users may be required to verify or confirm overtime records before approval by their reporting manager.
  6. Any corrections to attendance or timesheet records must be requested through the designated workflow in the Portal and are subject to manager or HR approval. The Company’s decision on disputed records shall be final.
  7. All attendance and timesheet entries will be governed by the Company’s attendance, leave, and payroll policies, as amended from time to time.
6. LEAVE MANAGEMENT
  1. All leave requests must be submitted exclusively through the Portal’s leave management module, specifying the type of leave (e.g., paid leave, casual leave, sick leave, unpaid leave, etc.) and the intended dates.
  2. No leave shall be deemed granted unless approved by the reporting manager and/or HR, in accordance with the Company’s leave policy. The Company reserves the right to approve, reject, or modify leave requests based on business requirements.
  3. In cases such as sick leave or other special leave categories, Users may be required to upload or submit supporting documentation (e.g., medical certificate, etc.) for approval.
  4. The Portal will display available leave balances for each User. It is the User’s responsibility to monitor their balances and plan accordingly. Leave taken in excess of the available balance may be treated as unpaid leave or result in salary deductions.
  5. In case of emergencies or unforeseen circumstances, the User must inform their reporting manager at the earliest possible time and subsequently update the leave record in the Portal.
  6. Official company holidays and weekly offs will be displayed in the Portal’s holiday calendar. Any absence adjacent to such days may be treated in accordance with the Company’s leave policies.
  7. Failure to follow the prescribed leave request and approval process may lead to the leave being marked as unauthorized absence, subject to disciplinary action and potential payroll impact.
